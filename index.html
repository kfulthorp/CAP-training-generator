How to Use This Generator

Use this generator together with the CPRS California Action Plan. Identify one CAP Strategy, Competency + Level (Have/Need), Trend, and Goal that will be the focus of your training. Then use this generator to create a draft training or CPRS conference session plan.
  1. Choose ONE Strategy, ONE Competency + Level (Have/Need), ONE Trend, and ONE Goal. You may select “N/A” for any category you don’t want to include.
  2. In Step 1, enter those selections and add local context, constraints, materials, and preferences.
  3. Change any field — the prompt in Step 2 auto-generates. You can also press the button.
  4. Copy the prompt from Step 2 and paste it into ChatGPT (or a similar AI).
  5. When ChatGPT returns your completed training plan, copy that text into Word or Google Docs for editing, or use “Download as Word (.doc)” / “Print / Save as PDF.”

CAP Quick Reference

Show CAP definitions used in the prompt
Strategies (with descriptions)
• Advocate — Influence public policy at the local, state, and federal levels to support parks and recreation.
• Communicate — Share the vision and value of parks and recreation with staff, leaders, stakeholders, partners, and the public to raise awareness of services and benefits.
• Evaluate — Research, document successes, and track performance to measure outcomes and inform public opinion and policy.
• Innovate — Evolve approaches and practices to respond to emerging trends and meet the needs of changing communities.
• Collaborate — Engage allied professionals, residents, educators, policymakers, sponsors, and service providers to leverage resources and maximize outcomes.

Goals (with descriptions)
• Promote Health and Wellness — Support physical, emotional, and mental health through fitness, play, and activity.
• Support Economic Vitality — Attract business, foster development, increase tourism, provide jobs, and generate revenues for community prosperity.
• Create Recreation Experiences — Increase community involvement through engaging programs, special events, and new recreation opportunities.
• Foster Social Cohesiveness — Build cohesive communities by celebrating diversity, providing inclusive services, promoting social equity, and ensuring access for all.
• Support Human Development — Advance youth development and lifelong learning to foster social, intellectual, physical, and emotional growth for all ages and abilities.
• Protect Natural Resources — Conserve habitat and open space, connect people to nature, and steward ecological functions of parklands.
• Strengthen Community Identity and Sense of Place — Provide parks, facilities, and services that reflect local character, heritage, culture, history, and landscape.
• Facilitate Community Problem Solving — Apply engagement, collaboration, leadership, and facilitation skills to address community issues.
• Support Community Vibrancy/Livability — Promote safe, vibrant, attractive, and walkable communities that make life better through parks and recreation.
